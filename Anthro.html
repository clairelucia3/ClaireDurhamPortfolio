Anthro email

When I think of Anthropologie, I think of self-expression, individuality, and authenticity. It isn’t just clothes or decor, but the people who bring it to life. I believe this is reflected in the name of Anthropologie. I wanted this email newsletter to feel deeply personal.

An inspiration of mine came from the ‘25/25 Fanatics video, where Victor Wembanyama introduces the NBA City-Edition Jerseys. In the video, he spins the narrative and puts ownership of the jersey in the hands of viewers. I wanted to convey a similiar message in the statement about Anthropologies inspiration. I wanted viewers to feel like part of the brand.

Playing along side Anthropologie's nostalgic and creative side, I transformed clothing into paper doll cutouts, a memorable childhood craft that I and many others share.

Another characteristic that I wanted to evoke was Anthropologie's playfulness. In the section featuring Home and Furniture, I wanted to make viewers laugh and feel good.

Usually when someone says you "look great in something," they are referring to clothes but viewers are met with a display of mirrors instead. I included a tag raeding #AReflectionOfYou, calling viewers to post a mirror selfie in their Anthropologie mirror.

Lastly, I wanted to play with colors and textures while keeping it all legible. Both bold and delicate, Anthropologie's use of fun colors and whimsical subjects have always inspired me.
