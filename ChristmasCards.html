Graphic Design

This section highlights my work in graphic design, including logos, brand guidelines, and marketing materials developed for clients and organizations.

Mama Mir's Kitchen
I had the opportunty to provide graphic design services to a local business called Mama Mir’s Kitchen that sold family-style meals and more. I designed content that reflects the brand’s warm, welcoming spirit and love for gathering around food. My goal was to capture a consistent mood of comfort and togetherness while highlighting the fresh, shareable meals that bring people closer.
While there, I developed a primary logo as well as a secondary logo. I defined the brands colors and typography. I also developed a brand guideline and many graphic templates that gave detailed instructions to be used so that the brand would remain consistent after I left the business. The owner prefered to work in Canva so I developed much of the graphics in Adobe Illustrator and transfered them into Canva for her to use later.
Olney Outlaws
The Olney Outlaws are LaSalle’s student section, created to bring energy, spirit, and a unified identity to gameday. In creating the Olney Outlaws logo, I aimed to capture a bold, western-inspired energy that embodies school spirit and toughness. The design blends rugged imagery with a modern edge, giving the student section a recognizable identity that feels both fierce and fun.
I worked with the Senior Design team at La Salle university in making the logo for the Outlaw organization. It was important to have a figure that was recognizable enough by La Salle students but different enough from the traditional La Salle explorer to ensure no laws were being broken.

A teammate and I worked to create this organization after feeling that our university sport teams lacked support from students. We recognized a divide between students and student-athletes. Few people attended games and teams did not support other teams. We looked at surrounding schools and saw that they had student section clubs so we knew we had to start our own. So we came up with the Olney Outlaws.

The club had a slow start the first year. There was little improvement to game attendance. However in the second year that we have lead the group, the numbers have multiplied and the club has recieved great positive feedback.
