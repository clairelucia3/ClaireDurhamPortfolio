UX/UI Design

This section showcases my UX/UI work, including projects built with HTML. Here, you’ll find examples of how I translate ideas into user-friendly layouts that balance clear navigation with engaging design.

Mama Mir's Kitchen

I was given the opportunity to build a website for a small business that sells meal-prepped food. I had more than a year experience working behind the counter of this business so I understood the business's values and clientele. I wanted to ensure that the website met the unique needs and wants of the customers through clear navigation and tools.

It was important that the website was easy to navigate and understand because convienance was very important to our customers.
To ensure this clarity, I included step by step instructions on how to order from the business.
One of the biggest challenges in building this website was weaving together the many sides of the business; food services, catering, rental opportunities, and employment, into one cohesive experience. To combat this, I made sure all sides of the business remained consistent with the brand, included seperate forms and pages.

It was also important that the website was directly linked to the system that keeps track of inventory, that way shoppers could see a live view of what is available.

Sam's Hope

This UX/UI project was created for an animal-focused non-profit called Sam's Hope with the goal of making information more accessible and encouraging user engagement. Working primarily in Figma, I focused on designing clear navigation, thoughtful layouts, and an interface that balances eye-catching colors with usability. The final design prioritizes ease of use while reflecting the organization’s mission of care, connection, and community impact.
I wanted the website to feel deepl personal to draw viewers to donate towards the non-profit. One challenge was providing the multitude of information and history without overwhelming the viewer with too many words. To combat this I made an interactive experience that reveals bits of information as the viewer moves through the page.
